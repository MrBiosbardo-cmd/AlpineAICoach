Overview

What is Alpine AI Coach?

Alpine AI Coach is a free, AI-powered cycling coaching assistant that runs directly inside Telegram. It was built by Carlos, a former Spanish professional cyclist, with one clear goal: to give every serious cyclist access to the kind of structured coaching and sport science support that used to be available only to funded athletes and professional teams.

Whether you are training for your first gran fondo, working toward a podium finish, or simply trying to ride smarter and recover better, Alpine AI Coach is designed to support your journey with personalised, evidence-based guidance.

The gap between amateur and professional cyclists is rarely about talent. It is almost always about access — to structured coaching, sport science knowledge, and consistent feedback. Alpine AI Coach exists to close that gap, making professional-level coaching support available to any motivated cyclist, anywhere in the world, completely free of charge.

Features

What Can Alpine AI Coach Do for You?

Alpine AI Coach provides a comprehensive set of coaching tools, all available through simple Telegram conversations:

  • Personalised training guidance based on your current fitness, goals, and ride history
  • Ride logging and analysis so your sessions are tracked and understood over time
  • Weekly performance feedback that helps you see progress and adjust your training
  • Sport science tips covering nutrition, recovery, pacing, and race preparation
  • RPE-based training that works without power meters, heart rate monitors, or any expensive equipment
  • 24/7 availability so you can log a ride or review your week at any time
How It Works

How Does Alpine AI Coach Work?

Alpine AI Coach runs entirely inside Telegram. There is nothing to download, no subscription to manage, and no app to install separately. Simply open your Telegram app and start a conversation with the bot.

The bot communicates with you in plain, clear language. You describe your rides, share how you felt, and Alpine AI Coach responds with coaching feedback tailored specifically to you.

Ride Logging

Logging Your Rides

The Basics: All You Need to Get Started

When you log a ride with Alpine AI Coach, the core information you need is simple:

  • Distance covered
  • Duration of the session
  • RPE — how hard the ride felt on a scale of 1 to 10

That is all you need. No technology required whatsoever.

If You Ride With a Basic Cycling Computer

If you have a basic cycling computer, you can enrich your ride log with additional data that helps Alpine AI Coach give you even more precise and personalised feedback. Useful metrics to include are:

  • Accumulated altitude — the total elevation gained during the ride, which gives important context about effort and workload. The same distance at RPE 7 on flat roads and in the mountains are two very different training sessions.
  • Average cadence — your average pedalling revolutions per minute, which helps the coach understand your riding style, efficiency, and whether your training is developing the neuromuscular patterns that match your goals.
  • Heart rate — if your cycling computer includes a heart rate monitor, you can manually enter your Average HR and/or Maximum HR for the session. This adds valuable physiological context to your ride data and allows Alpine AI Coach to give you deeper, more accurate feedback.

You do not need a GPS device or a smartphone to use Alpine AI Coach effectively. But if your computer records these numbers, sharing them makes your coaching data richer and your feedback more accurate.

A note on heart rate monitoring: A heart rate monitor is not compulsory. RPE is the foundation of how Alpine AI Coach analyses your training, and it works completely on its own. Heart rate data, when available, adds an extra layer of precision to the analysis — but it is always supplementary, never a requirement.

If You Ride With a Smartphone or GPS Device

If you ride with a smartphone app such as Strava, Komoot, or similar, or with a Garmin device or any equivalent GPS cycling computer, you can upload your ride file directly to Alpine AI Coach.

Supported file formats are:

  • .fit — the standard file format used by Garmin and most GPS cycling devices
  • .gpx — a widely supported format used by smartphones and many cycling apps

Simply export the file from your device or app after your ride and upload it directly in your Telegram conversation with Alpine AI Coach.

Note on Device Data and File Uploads

If your device records GPS and movement data, file uploads will import ride metrics including distance, duration, elevation, speed, cadence, and GPS route.

Heart rate data: Where heart rate is present in your file, it will be fully used by the coach, including average HR, max HR, and HR drift analysis. You can also log HR manually after any session.

Power meter data: Advanced metrics derived from power output, such as normalised power, training stress score, and intensity factor, will be available in a future update. Alpine AI Coach will continue to expand its data processing capabilities over time.

Core Method

Understanding RPE: Your Most Important Tool

Alpine AI Coach uses RPE, which stands for Rate of Perceived Exertion, as the primary method for logging ride intensity. RPE is a 1 to 10 scale that measures how hard your ride feels. Professional coaches have used it for decades, and it works without any technology at all.

RPE Description
1Very easy. Recovery spin, barely any effort.
2Very light. Easy warm-up pace.
3Easy. Long endurance ride, fully comfortable.
4Moderate easy. Steady pace, breathing slightly elevated.
5Moderate. Tempo effort, can speak in short phrases.
6Moderate hard. Sustained effort, conversation is difficult.
7Hard. Threshold pace, breathing heavy and controlled.
8Very hard. Race pace or hard interval effort.
9Extremely hard. Close to your absolute limit.
10Maximum. Sprint finish or all-out effort only.

How to use it: After each ride, ask yourself how hard the session felt overall — not just the hardest moment. That number is your RPE. Be honest with yourself. There is no right or wrong answer, and accuracy over time is what matters.

Alpine AI Coach uses your RPE data to understand your training load, monitor fatigue, and give you better guidance as your fitness evolves. Heart rate data, where available, adds depth to that picture — but RPE is always the foundation.

Follow these steps to begin your first session with Alpine AI Coach:

  1. 1Open Telegram and search for the Alpine AI Coach bot
